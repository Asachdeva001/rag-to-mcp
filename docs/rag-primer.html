Slide 1 of 8 · Context

Why LLMs fail on your data

Three structural problems — one solution

Knowledge cutoff
LLMs are trained on data up to a fixed date. They know nothing about documents created after that date — or documents that were never public.
Private data blindness
Your HR policy, IT acceptable use policy, and finance reimbursement rules are not on the internet. The LLM has never seen them.
Context window limits
You cannot paste all your documents into every prompt. The context window has a limit — and filling it with irrelevant text degrades answer quality.
The RAG solution
Retrieve only the relevant document chunks at query time. Give the LLM exactly what it needs — no more. Enforce that it answers from retrieved context only.
"RAG is not about making the LLM smarter. It is about giving it the right context — and nothing else."

Slide 3 of 8 · Core Concept

What is an embedding?

A list of numbers that represents meaning — similar meaning = close numbers

Query: "Who approves leave without pay?"
[0.41, -0.12, 0.67, ...] 384 values
Chunk: "LWP requires approval from the Department Head and the HR Director..."
score: 0.84 ✓
Chunk: "Annual leave accrues at 1.5 days per month from the date of joining..."
score: 0.31 ✗
"The embedding model (all-MiniLM-L6-v2) converts every sentence into 384 numbers. Your query and the matching chunk end up close in that 384-dimensional space. That closeness is the score."
Slide 4 of 8 · Failure Mode 1

Why chunking strategy matters

Fixed-size chunking vs sentence-aware chunking — on clause 5.2

❌ Fixed-size (400 chars)
Chunk 11: "...An employee may apply for Leave Without Pay only after exhausting all applicable paid leave entitlements. LWP requires approval from the Department Head and the"
Chunk 12: "HR Director. Manager approval alone is not sufficient. LWP exceeding 30 continuous days requires approval from the Municipal Commissioner..."
→ Query "who approves LWP" retrieves chunk 11
→ Answer: "Department Head" — HR Director missing
→ Condition drop
✅ Sentence-aware
Chunk 11: "An employee may apply for Leave Without Pay only after exhausting all applicable paid leave entitlements. LWP requires approval from the Department Head and the HR Director. Manager approval alone is not sufficient."
→ Both conditions in the same chunk
→ Answer: "Department Head AND HR Director"
→ Complete obligation preserved
"Your Enforcement rule: chunk size must not exceed 400 tokens. Never split mid-sentence. This single rule prevents the chunk boundary failure."

How retrieval works — and when it fails

Cosine similarity on vectors — not keyword search

embedded → [0.31, 0.77, -0.12, ...]

score 0.72 — policy_hr_leave.txt chunk 3

score 0.68 — policy_hr_leave.txt chunk 8
"Male employees are entitled to 5 days paternity..."
HR leave chunks returned for an IT question
✅ With threshold (0.6) + doc filter
score 0.81 — policy_it_acceptable_use.txt chunk 4
"Personal devices may be used to access CMC email and the employee self-service portal only..."
Correct document, correct chunk, cited

Slide 6 of 8 · Failure Mode 3

Grounding the answer

The LLM still has its training knowledge — Enforcement keeps it out

Retrieved context (what the LLM sees)
[policy_it_acceptable_use.txt, chunk 4]: "Personal devices may be used to access CMC email and the CMC employee self-service portal only. Personal devices must not be used to access, store, or transmit classified or sensitive CMC data."
❌ No grounding enforcement
"Yes, you can use your personal phone for approved work tools and email. As is standard practice in government organisations, employees are expected to maintain appropriate security on personal devices."
↑ "standard practice" — not in any document. LLM used training knowledge.
✅ With grounding enforcement
"Personal devices may be used to access CMC email and the CMC employee self-service portal only. [Source: policy_it_acceptable_use.txt, chunk 4]"
↑ Every word traceable to the retrieved chunk.
Enforcement rule: "Answer must use only information present in the retrieved chunks. Never add context from outside the retrieved set. If the answer is not in retrieved chunks — use the refusal template."
Slide 7 of 8 · Summary

The three failure modes

All caused by missing Enforcement rules — all fixable in agents.md

1
Chunk boundary failure
Fixed-size chunking splits multi-condition clauses across chunks. Retrieval returns an incomplete clause. Answer drops one condition silently. Fix: sentence-aware chunking + max 400 token limit.
2
Wrong chunk retrieval
No similarity threshold or document metadata filter. Query "personal phone" retrieves HR leave chunks with superficial similarity. Fix: threshold 0.6 + document-level filter in Enforcement.
3
Answer outside retrieved context
LLM uses training knowledge to enrich the answer. Adds "as is standard practice" — not in any document. Fix: grounding Enforcement rule requiring every claim to be traceable to a retrieved chunk.
"All three failures are invisible without a systematic check. Your agents.md Enforcement rules are the check."
